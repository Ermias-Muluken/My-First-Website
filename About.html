Understanding Chess

Chess Pieces

Pawn

A pawn may move by vertically advancing to a vacant square ahead. The first time a pawn moves, it has the additional option of vertically advancing two squares, provided that both squares are vacant. Unlike other pieces, the pawn can only move forwards. In the below diagram, the pawn on c4 can only move to c5; the pawn on e2 can move to either e3 or e4. When capturing the opponent piece it can move diagonally on step. If the white reaches the top row it can be promoted to any other pieces of the same color except the pawn itself and king. Same for black pawns that reaches the bottom row.

Night

A Night move in a lit bit complicated way compared to the other pieces and that makes it special. It moves in “L” shape i.e. It can move one square vertical then two squares horizontal or two square vertical then one squares horizontal to every direction contained in the board. It can move to any empty space whether there is any piece on the way or not. and to move and capture is the same. I think this feature makes Night so unique and so powerful in some positions. By the way Night is my favorite piece.

Bishop

A bishop moves diagonally following its position color. i.e. If there is a bishop on black square that means that bishop can only go to black squares following diagonal direction. It can not pass a piece on the way. If the piece on the way is your opponent piece you can capture that piece and settle there.

Rook

A rook can move in any straight line direction whether it is horizontal or vertical direction. It moves and captures in same manner. When countering pieces on the way if it is opponent piece it can capture it and settle there or if it the same color piece it can’t pass it so if there is empty squares before the occupied square it can settle there.

Queen

A queen can move both diagonally and in any straight line direction, i.e. It can move like a bishop or a rook. The movement rules that apply to the bishop and the rook apply to the queen too. Since the queen can easily move the entire board easily it the second most powerful piece on the board next to the king.

King

The king is most powerful and most prioritized piece among the chess pieces. It is due to the concept of the chess game is based on which is the “capture” of the king or checkmating the king. So in chess your main aim should be protecting your king and mobilizing your pieces to checkmate the opponent king. The king can move in all directions one square inside the board. and it move and capture in the same manner.

Concepts and Rules

The main concept in the chess i think is check and checkmate. Where check means that placing your one of your piece(except the king) to a place where it is possible for the piece that is placed in that position to capture the opponent king. when you put your piece in that position it is called check. And if the opponent can't do anything to not be captured by that piece then it is called checkmate.

The Game over rule is simple if you can checkmate your king you have won. If any of the opponent king is not possible to be checkmated in a fair game then it is draw. But there are still some rules that you should know like un'passant or you can't move your king to a square where it is possible for your opponent pieces to go that position.
